# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business stakeholders and technical teams. They gather requirements, document process gaps, analyze impacts, and translate business needs into actionable specifications.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct stakeholder interviews and requirements elicitation
- Document business processes and identify bottlenecks
- Translate stakeholder needs into clear, actionable specifications
- Assess business impact of proposed changes and solutions
- Facilitate workshops and alignment sessions with cross-functional teams
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business objectives

### Goals
- Ensure technical solutions align with business objectives
- Reduce requirement ambiguity and scope creep
- Improve stakeholder satisfaction through clear communication
- Identify process improvements and efficiency opportunities

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and business case analysis
- Stakeholder workshops and requirements review sessions
- Collaboration with Product Managers on feature prioritization
- Input to Project Managers on scope and dependencies

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers advocate for user experience by designing intuitive interfaces, conducting usability research, and validating that solutions meet user needs. They collaborate closely with product and engineering to ensure customer-centric design.

### Responsibilities
- Design user flows, wireframes, and prototypes
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Provide design feedback during planning and code reviews
- Ensure consistency in UI/UX patterns and accessibility standards
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria for user-facing features
- Validate designs through user testing and iteration

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support costs
- Ensure design consistency across products
- Validate design decisions with user research and data

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and usability reports
- Design specs and interactive prototypes
- Collaboration with Product Managers and Developers on trade-offs

---

## Stakeholder Representatives

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Representatives provide ongoing feedback and validation from the perspective of end users, business units, or subject matter experts. They ensure deliverables meet real-world needs and expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Attend demos and provide timely feedback
- Review and validate deliverables against business requirements
- Clarify requirements and edge cases as a subject matter expert
- Represent the voice of customers or business units
- Participate in acceptance testing and sign-off
- Provide input on prioritization and trade-offs

### Goals
- Ensure solutions meet end-user and business needs
- Reduce rework by providing early and frequent feedback
- Improve product-market fit and adoption
- Bridge communication between delivery teams and business

### Typical Communication
- Sprint demos and milestone reviews
- Feedback sessions and acceptance testing
- Input on requirements and prioritization discussions
- Escalation of concerns to Product and Project Managers

---

## Release Managers

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate release timelines, deployment communication, and post-launch support. They ensure smooth, predictable releases and minimize deployment risk.

### Responsibilities
- Manage release schedules and deployment windows
- Coordinate cross-team dependencies for releases
- Develop and maintain deployment checklists and runbooks
- Communicate release plans to stakeholders and support teams
- Oversee post-deployment verification and incident response
- Track release metrics and identify improvement opportunities

### Goals
- Deliver predictable, low-risk releases
- Minimize deployment-related incidents and downtime
- Ensure clear communication and coordination across teams
- Continuously improve release processes and automation

### Typical Communication
- Release calendars and deployment schedules
- Pre-release readiness reviews with engineering and stakeholders
- Post-deployment status updates and metrics
- Coordination with DevOps Engineers and Project Managers

---

## DevOps Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers own infrastructure automation, CI/CD pipelines, and deployment reliability. They enable fast, safe delivery by building robust development and production environments.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines and automation
- Configure and monitor infrastructure and environments
- Troubleshoot build, test, and deployment issues
- Implement security scanning and compliance checks
- Optimize deployment speed and reliability
- Provide tooling and observability for development teams

### Goals
- Enable fast, automated, and reliable deployments
- Reduce toil through automation and self-service tooling
- Ensure infrastructure security and compliance
- Improve observability and incident response times

### Typical Communication
- Infrastructure design docs and runbooks
- Pipeline status and deployment metrics
- Incident response and post-mortems
- Collaboration with Developers, Release Managers, and Project Managers
